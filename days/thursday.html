🔍 Thursday - The Final Challenge

8 May 2026 - by Rubeen De Roose

Thursday was the last day of the CTF, and it could be felt from the moment you walked in. The groups sitting at the top of the leaderboard were on edge and it made for an interesting dynamic throughout the day. People were attempting to get information out of each other in all sorts of ways, some more straightforward than others. It was interesting to see how far people were willing to go when there were points on the line, and honestly it was entertaining to watch in the background when you were not the one being questioned.

The room on the final day of the CTF.

The challenges for this day had a larger focus on finding clues or flags in the real world instead of solving digital challenges, the most interesting of which had us searching the bathrooms for hidden images. These then had to be identified by their font type, the kind of challenge that sounds simple until you are actually standing in a bathroom trying to figure out what you are even looking for. We were getting strange looks from students who had no idea what 15 people could possibly need from the same bathroom hallway. There was a lot of back and forth between team members trying to piece things together, which eventually led us to the flag for that challenge.

One of the hidden images found during the bathroom challenge.

Around midday we headed to the school cafeteria for lunch, curious to see what students were offered for food at the university. What surprised us was not just the price but the quality for 13 euros the meals looked as though they would be served in a proper restaurant. It was something we would not have expected from a university cafeteria, and came as a welcome surprise.

Everyone was chasing whatever points they could still grab before the challenges officially closed, and the urgency could be felt in the room. Teams moved faster, conversations were shorter, and the relaxed atmosphere from the start of the week could no longer be felt. When the challenges closed at 4pm, the Godslayers, who had been one of the top performing teams throughout the entire week, took first place on the leaderboard.

To wind down from the project week, we headed to the city center to enjoy the good weather and sample some of Sweden's ciders, with a view of the local church to close out the day.

Winding down in the city center with a view of the local church.
